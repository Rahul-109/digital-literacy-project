Case Study of Cybercrime: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a prevalent form of cybercrime in which perpetrators attempt to deceive victims into divulging private information like passwords, bank account information, or one-time passwords. Fake emails, messages, or websites that seem to be from reliable sources, such as banks or well-known businesses, are typically used to accomplish this.

The victim of a typical phishing attack gets a message saying that they have won a prize or that there is an urgent problem with their bank account. A link in the message takes the user to a phoney website that mimics the real one. The attacker obtains unauthorised access to the user's account by capturing their login credentials or OTP.

Mostly students and online user gets targeted by scammers as they use digital platforms and may not verify the links carefully. The consequences of this include info theft , financial loss , misuse of personal information.Phishing attacks are increasing rapidly, making it important for users to stay alert and verify any suspicious communication before taking action.
